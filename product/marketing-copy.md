# Teams Trivia — Marketing Copy

---

## For Players

### Think fast. Answer right. Stay alive.

Your phone is your buzzer. No app to download, no account to create — just scan the QR code on screen, pick a name, and you're in.

When the question drops, you've got **10 seconds**. Four options. One shot. Tap the right answer and you survive. Tap the wrong one — or hesitate too long — and you're out.

After every question the big screen shows exactly how the room voted. Then the correct answer is revealed and the survivors are counted. Watch the field thin round by round until only the sharpest players are left standing.

Got knocked out? No worries — stay and watch. Cheer on who's left, see how the group voted, and find out if you'd have made it.

**No setup. No teams. Just you, your instincts, and a countdown.**

---

## For Presenters & Admins

### Run a show-stopping moment in minutes.

Teams Trivia turns any all-hands, offsite, or team event into a live game show — and it takes about five minutes to set up.

**Before the event:**
Write your questions in a spreadsheet — anything from company trivia and product knowledge to fun icebreakers. Save it as a CSV, and you're ready.

**At the event:**
1. Open the admin panel and upload your question file.
2. A QR code and join link appear on the broadcast screen. Point the audience at it and watch them join in real time.
3. Start the game when you're ready. You control the pace — advance to the next question whenever the moment is right.

**During the game:**
The broadcast screen handles everything your audience sees: question text, the live countdown, and a breakdown of how everyone voted before the answer is revealed. You see live answer stats on your screen so you know when the room has settled.

**No tech support needed.** Players join on their own devices — phones, laptops, tablets — and the game works the same whether your audience is in the room, on a call, or split across both.

Run it as a warm-up, a closing act, or the centrepiece of your event. Either way, the last person standing will be talking about it for weeks.
